Research

I study how stable density stratification changes the structure and evolution of wakes. My work combines experimental fluid mechanics with data-driven, interpretable analysis to extract coherent dynamics and identify wake regimes across parameter space. I am interested in methods that connect physical mechanisms to measurements and support robust inference in geophysical and environmental flow contexts.

Theme 1: Stratified Wakes and Near-Wake Physics

In stratified environments, wakes evolve under competing effects of buoyancy, momentum, and viscosity. I use controlled experiments to quantify how Reynolds number, Froude number, and body incidence modify wake spacing, separation, and vortex organization.

Methods

  • Controlled towing-tank experiments
  • Parameter sweeps in Reynolds and Froude space
  • Comparative scaling and dimensionless wake metrics

Representative Work

Ohh and Spedding (2024), Journal of Fluid Mechanics.

Theme 2: Modal Decomposition and Interpretable Signatures

I use dynamic mode decomposition and related methods to extract energetic coherent structures from experimental and numerical datasets. The goal is to build compact, interpretable descriptors that support automated wake identification and cross-condition comparison.

Methods

  • Dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) and variants
  • Feature selection from energetic mode content
  • Cross-validation across simulation and experiment

Representative Work

Ohh and Spedding (2022), Physical Review Fluids.

Theme 3: Regime Identification from Limited Measurements

Many practical settings permit only partial sensing. I work on sparse modeling strategies that identify regimes using physically meaningful features rather than black-box predictors, improving interpretability and transferability.

Methods

  • Library-based sparse regression
  • Reduced-sensing regime classification
  • Validation against known regime maps

Representative Work

Chinta, Ohh, Spedding, and Luhar (2022), Physical Review Fluids.

Future Directions

  • Identify minimal measurement sets that reliably distinguish stratified wake regimes.
  • Link near-wake structure to far-wake signatures and internal-wave radiation.
  • Develop uncertainty-aware, interpretable models for regime identification from observations.
