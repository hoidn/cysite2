## Research Page (Overview)
I study how stable density stratification changes the structure and evolution of wakes. My work combines experimental fluid mechanics with data-driven, interpretable analysis to extract coherent dynamics and identify wake regimes across parameter space. I’m interested in methods that connect physical mechanisms to measurements and enable robust inference in geophysical and environmental flow contexts.

## Research Theme 1 (Paragraph)
**Stratified wakes and near-wake physics.** In stratified environments, wakes evolve under competing effects of buoyancy, momentum, and viscosity, producing distinct regimes and signatures. I use controlled experiments to quantify how parameters such as Reynolds number, Froude number, and body incidence modify wake spacing, separation, and vortex organization, and how inclined wakes can generate large-scale internal-wave undulations. A key goal is to connect near-wake geometry to downstream evolution through comparable, dimensionless metrics across different body shapes and flow conditions.

## Research Theme 2 (Paragraph)
**Modal decomposition and interpretable signatures (DMD).** I use dynamic mode decomposition and related approaches to extract energetic coherent structures from experimental and numerical datasets. By selecting informative modal content, I develop compact, interpretable descriptors that support automated wake identification and comparison across conditions. I’m particularly interested in understanding when classification succeeds or fails as data quality and measurement dimensionality change, and in building analysis pipelines that remain robust and transparent.

## Research Theme 3 (Paragraph)
**Regime identification from limited measurements.** Many practical settings allow only partial sensing of a wake. I work on reduced-measurement and sparse modeling strategies that identify regimes using physically meaningful features, rather than black-box predictors. This includes library-based sparse regression formulations that map limited observations to regime structure while preserving interpretability and supporting validation against known regime maps.

## Selected Publication Summaries (Prose)

### JFM (2024) — Stratification effects in the near wake of a prolate spheroid
We performed experiments on the near wake of a 6:1 prolate spheroid in uniform and stably stratified backgrounds, varying pitch angle to isolate how geometry and incidence influence wake formation. We quantify how stratification modifies characteristic wake spacing and induces internal-wave undulations, and we show that later-stage wake evolution converges toward established stratified-wake behavior.

### Physical Review Fluids (2022) — DMD for stratified wake identification
We use dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) to extract energetic modes that characterize stratified wakes and propose an algorithm that sorts and classifies wake regimes using a compact modal signature. The results highlight when automated classification is very successful and when it degrades as measurement quality or dimensionality changes, pointing to practical requirements for data-driven identification.

### Physical Review Fluids (2022) — Limited-measurement regime identification via sparse regression
We develop a sparse-regression framework that identifies stratified wake regimes from limited measurements by fitting observed dynamics to a library of candidate features. The approach targets scenarios where only partial sensing is available and emphasizes interpretability tied to underlying wake structure.
