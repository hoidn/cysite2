### 2024 — Journal of Fluid Mechanics
Citation:
Ohh, C.-Y. and Spedding, G. R. (2024). *The effects of stratification on the near wake of 6:1 prolate spheroid.* Journal of Fluid Mechanics, 997, A43.

Summary draft:
We performed experiments on the near wake of a 6:1 prolate spheroid in uniform and stably stratified backgrounds, varying pitch angle to isolate how geometry and incidence influence wake formation. We quantify how stratification modifies characteristic wake spacing and induces internal-wave undulations, while showing that later-stage wake evolution converges toward previously observed stratified-wake behavior.

### 2022 — Physical Review Fluids (Editors’ Suggestion)
Citation:
Ohh, C.-Y. and Spedding, G. R. (2022). *Wake identification of stratified flows using dynamic mode decomposition.* Physical Review Fluids, 7, 024801.

Summary draft:
We use dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) to extract energetic modes that characterize stratified wakes and propose an algorithm that sorts and classifies wake regimes using a compact modal signature. The results show when and how automated classification succeeds, and they outline a path toward data-driven identification in more complex, fully turbulent wakes.

Suggested thumbnail:
- `cy_ohh_assets/figures/PRF_2022_024801_Fig6_snapshots_DMD_modes.png`

Links:
- DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevFluids.7.024801
- PDF: `cy_ohh_assets/pubs/Ohh_Spedding_2022_PRF_024801.pdf`

### 2022 — Physical Review Fluids (with Chinta, Spedding, Luhar)
Citation:
Chinta, V. K., Ohh, C.-Y., Spedding, G., and Luhar, M. (2022). *Regime identification for stratified wakes from limited measurements: a library-based sparse regression formulation.* Physical Review Fluids, 7, 033803.

Summary draft:
We develop a sparse-regression framework that identifies stratified wake regimes from limited measurements by fitting observed dynamics to a library of candidate features. This approach targets practical scenarios where only partial sensing is available, supporting interpretable classification tied to physical structure.

Links:
- DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevFluids.7.033803
- arXiv: 2202.04119

### 2016 — APS DFD Abstract
Citation:
Ohh, C.-Y., Huang, Y., Wen, Z., Kanso, E., and Luhar, M. (2016). *Leveraging fluid-structure interaction for passive control of flapping locomotion.* APS DFD Meeting abstract.

Summary draft:
We studied how passive fluid-structure interactions can stabilize and shape flapping-locomotion dynamics, using experiments and numerical validation on an oscillated rigid “Lambda-flyer.” We mapped distinct behaviors including periodic rotation, chaotic dynamics, and bistability across dimensionless parameter ranges.

PDF:
- `cy_ohh_assets/pubs/Ohh_et_al_2016_APS_DFD_Abstract_001142.pdf`
